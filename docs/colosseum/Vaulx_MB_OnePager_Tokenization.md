## What is Vaulx

**Vaulx is a Solana on-chain credit protocol** that connects asset-rich individuals in high-rate markets to global onchain capital — secured by verifiable physical luxury collateral with deterministic on-chain liquidation.

Live on Solana Devnet today: 4 Anchor programs, 45+ tests passing, end-to-end demo at vaulx.vercel.app.

---

## The opportunity

A São Paulo Rolex owner has three credit options — *rotativo* at ~400% APR, consumer loans at ~60%, or Caixa *penhor* at ~30% APR with 20% LTV at scrap-metal valuation. Meanwhile, onchain USDC liquidity sits idle at 8%. **Vaulx is the rail between them.**

We orchestrate licensed off-chain counterparties — independent custody, Lloyd's-class insurance, certified appraisal — and route the loan through Solana smart contracts that release USDC only after the custodian signs custody-confirmation atomically on-chain. Borrowers get 50% LTV at 24% APR (2%/month) — cheaper than Caixa, with 2.5× more capital. LPs earn 8% senior or 12% junior tranches.

The single missing piece for our Brazil launch is a **regulated tokenization partner**.

Vaulx originates and operates the protocol; MB legally issues the tokenized claim; the cNFT on Solana mirrors the regulated record.

## Why this is interesting for MB

1. **A new RWA vertical** — luxury physical collateral, differentiated from the agribusiness-receivables and real-estate-debt issuances that dominate the BR tokenization market.
2. **A Solana foothold** — most Brazilian RWA today sits on Polygon. Solana's institutional adoption is accelerating: $1.82B RWA TVL crossed in March, Western Union launched USDPT this month, BlackRock and Franklin Templeton money funds are live. MB establishing a Solana presence through Vaulx is timely.
3. **Recurring revenue** — origination fees per asset issued + ongoing share of protocol revenue on BR-issued loans + FX spread on BRL/USDC settlement. Combined ~$200–300K/year recurring at $10M originated.
4. **LP-side opportunity** — Vaulx senior tranche at 8% APR (USDC, physical-collateral-backed, Lloyd's-insured, with 5% POL first-loss buffer below) is competitive vs Maple syrupUSDC at ~7% and structurally attractive vs BR fixed income for MB's institutional clients.
