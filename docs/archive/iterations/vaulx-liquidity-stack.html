Liquidity Architecture

Who brings capital — and how

Neither Kamino nor Plume supplies balance sheet capital to Vaulx. They supply venue infrastructure. The first $5–10M in lending liquidity comes from 2–3 manually closed relationships: a vault curator, an anchor institutional lender, and optionally a crypto-native credit fund.

The hard truth: Protocols provide rails. You bring the borrowers, collateral infrastructure, and the first anchor lenders. Liquidity follows a track record of zero bad debt.

What each protocol actually gives you

Infrastructure vs. capital — the distinction that matters

Kamino Finance

Provides isolated market architecture + vault/curator distribution rails. The $2.45B TVL belongs to Kamino's existing lenders — it does not auto-allocate to new markets.


A curator (Re7, MEV Capital, Steakhouse) must choose to allocate USDC into a Vaulx Kamino market. That choice requires a verified oracle, a credible liquidation mechanism for cNFT collateral, and a competitive APY vs. Maple/OnRe/Solend.


✓ Use now: venue to scale after track record exists.

Plume Network

Provides institutional RWA yield infrastructure — Apollo ($50M), Solv ($10M), Hamilton Lane deployed through Plume's own curated vaults.


Those LPs are locked to Plume-native vaults. Plume does not redirect institutional capital to third-party protocols as a default. What Plume gives Vaulx is: a credible later-stage venue for TRDCs in secondary lending markets + RWA-native chain infrastructure for cross-chain expansion.


✗ Phase 2 only. Premature to rely on for launch liquidity.

Order book credit market, launched first BRL credit market on Solana (Transfero/Etherfuse, March 2026). First permissioned RWA credit product using Apollo ACRED as collateral. Lenders set bilateral terms — a single large lender can fill an entire market without retail depositor base.

Kamino V2 Isolated Market VENUE — PARALLEL
Isolated market architecture with custom LTV, collateral rules, and liquidation logic per market. $2.45B TVL provides deep lender network, but requires curator adoption for a new market to receive USDC inflows.
$2.45B TVL base — post-track-record scale venue
Why Vaulx: Once 6–12 months of zero bad debt exist, curator allocation becomes achievable. Launch the market now to establish the Vaulx product identity, even if inflows are low initially.

Managed USDC pools that allocate across Kamino V2 markets — your bridge to pooled institutional capital
Re7 Labs CURATOR — PRIORITY
Specialises in novel collateral types and new DeFi market segments. Active curator on Kamino V2 and Morpho. Known for willingness to evaluate non-standard collateral with strong legal wrapping.

MakerDAO-ecosystem curator that manages RWA credit vaults. More conservative risk profile — prefers sovereign or near-sovereign collateral.

Mercado Bitcoin ANCHOR LENDER — PRIORITY
Brazil's largest crypto exchange with regulated securities arm (MB Tokens / MB Digital Assets). Their users — HNW Brazilians with physical luxury assets — are Vaulx's exact borrower profile. Already issues tokenized CRAs and debentures.
Natural lender base for CCB watch-backed product

Issued BRZ stablecoin. Active in Solana RWA stack (Loopscale BRL credit market, March 2026). Understands Brazilian regulatory structure and has institutional relationships with family offices and crypto funds.
Deal structure: BRZ-denominated lending pool in Loopscale's Vaulx market. Transfero earns yield; Vaulx gets BRL-native liquidity without FX friction for Brazilian borrowers.
Credora / Maple Finance CREDIT FUND — MONITOR
Credora provides on-chain credit intelligence for institutional lending. Maple Finance (syrupUSDC on Solana) brings its own LP base and deployed $30M seed + $500K incentives on Kamino for launch.
Why Vaulx: Maple's playbook is the reference model — institutional-grade, permissioned. Engage after the first Loopscale market cohort has 90-day public data. Credora can provide credit scoring for the cNFT collateral pool.

Largest EVM lending protocol (~$15B TVL). Governance-controlled asset onboarding requires months of proposals, risk framework audits, and community votes. EVM-native — no Solana market.
Why not now: EVM-only, slow governance, no BRL/Brazil specificity, no RWA/cNFT precedent. If Vaulx expands to EVM (Base, Arbitrum) in Phase 2, Aave becomes relevant. Ignore for Solana launch.
