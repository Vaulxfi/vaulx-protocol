Vaulx / Vaulx — Internal Strategy

Liquidity does not come from
protocols. It comes from relationships.

Kamino and Plume provide infrastructure rails — isolated market venues and institutional distribution layers respectively. The lending capital itself must be sourced and closed manually through a 3-tier partner stack, sequenced by phase.

Core principle

"Kamino's $2.45B TVL belongs to Kamino's existing lenders. Plume's institutional capital flows into Plume's own curated vaults. Neither redirects balance sheet to third-party protocols."

The protocols provide market infrastructure — isolated collateral vaults, curator frameworks, institutional distribution rails. Vaulx bootstraps both sides of the lending market by closing 2–3 anchor relationships manually: one vault curator (Re7 Labs or MEV Capital), one Brazilian institutional lender (Mercado Bitcoin or Transfero), and one crypto-native credit facility.

AAVE NOT NOW

EVM-native. DAO governance onboarding takes months. No Brazil-specific liquidity. No Solana native. Novel collateral whitelisting requires full governance proposal cycle.

Verdict: Wrong stack, wrong timeline, wrong jurisdiction. Revisit only if EVM bridge strategy becomes relevant in Phase 3.

Why not AAVE

AAVE operates on EVM chains (Ethereum, Arbitrum, Base, Polygon). Vaulx is Solana-native with CCB legal structure governed by Brazilian law. Cross-chain bridging adds regulatory and technical complexity with no benefit at launch. AAVE's governance timeline for novel RWA collateral whitelisting runs 3–6 months minimum — longer than the Solana hackathon window. The Solana-native stack (Loopscale + Kamino V2) is faster, more composable, and more aligned with the CCB settlement architecture.
